About this backtest Not a prediction

This is history, not a forecast. For every starting year from 1871 onward, this replays your plan using that year's actual market returns and inflation. "Success rate" is the count of those past periods that met your goal — it is not a probability that your plan will succeed. Raising the goal ending amount above $0 makes success stricter (you're asking the plan to also leave a margin), but it's still a tally of what did happen, never a forecast of what will.

Past results do not predict future returns. The U.S. market has had 121 distinct 30-year starting windows; the next 30 years will be a 122nd, and it is not in this dataset. Backtests systematically miss tail risks that haven't happened yet.

Known simplifications. Tax brackets are held at 2026 levels across the entire 1871–2025 replay — historical brackets are not reconstructed, so any income tax treatment is approximate. Returns are nominal index returns before fees and fund-specific costs; your real portfolio diverges based on expense ratios and asset location. See Settings → Disclosures for the full assumption set.
