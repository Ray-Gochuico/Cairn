What does APR stand for?

✓ Correct

APR (Annual Percentage Rate) is the yearly cost of borrowing, including interest plus most fees, expressed as a single rate. When you're earning rather than borrowing, compare APY instead, since it accounts for compounding.

Advanced Roth · conversions · tax

A backdoor Roth IRA is subject to which IRS rule that can create unexpected taxes if you hold pre-tax IRA balances?

✕ Not quite — the answer is C, the pro-rata rule

The pro-rata rule treats all your non-Roth IRAs (Traditional, SEP, SIMPLE) as one pot. The taxable fraction of a conversion equals pre-tax balance ÷ total balance — so a large pre-tax balance makes a "backdoor" conversion partly taxable. The usual fix is rolling pre-tax IRA dollars into a 401(k) first, isolating only the after-tax basis.

Read more about the Pro-rata rule →

Source: IRS Pub 590-A & 590-B
