How your paycheck is calculated

Four things come out between gross and take-home. Here's the order Cairn applies them.

1 Pre-tax deductions come off first

Your 401(k), HSA, FSA, and health premiums are subtracted before any tax is figured, which lowers the income your federal and state taxes are calculated on. That's why contributing more here can raise take-home by less than you put in — you're trading taxed cash for an untaxed balance.

2 Federal withholding

We estimate federal income tax from the 2026 brackets using your filing status, dependents, and the standard deduction. Withholding is an estimate of what you'll owe — your actual refund or bill is settled when you file. There are no W-4 "allowances" anymore; dependents and any extra dollar amount do that job now.

3 FICA — Social Security & Medicare

FICA is a flat payroll tax: 6.2% for Social Security (up to the annual wage base) and 1.45% for Medicare. High earners pay an extra 0.9% Additional Medicare on wages above $200,000 (single) / $250,000 (married filing jointly) — we surface that line only when it applies.

4 State & local income tax

State tax varies widely — some states (Texas, Washington, Florida) have none, while others layer on city or county levies. We estimate from each jurisdiction's own brackets and standard deduction. When a state has no income tax, that row reads "no state income tax" rather than $0.

These figures are estimates for planning, not tax advice. Brackets reflect the 2026 tax year and are bundled with the app — verify against your pay stub or a tax professional before acting. Read Cairn's full estimate disclosure →
What this calculator does NOT model
  • State Disability Insurance (CA SDI, NJ SDI, NY DBL) and state PFML deductions — withheld on top of state tax.
  • FICA wage-base nuance on cafeteria-plan deferrals (Cairn slightly over-collects FICA here, matching SmartAsset's simplification).
  • AMT on ISO exercises landing in the same period.
  • Mid-year HSA / FSA contribution changes — we apply your current monthly election across the full year.
  • Local rules for the ~250 city/county jurisdictions are estimates; unusual residency splits aren't handled.
